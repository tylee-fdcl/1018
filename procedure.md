# Procedure for Starting a Merit Badge

## Merit Badge Counselor

The role of a Merit Badge Counselor is to provide guidance, mentoring, and evaluation as Scouts work through the merit badge requirements. The counselor does not function
as a formal instructor. Scouts are expected to review the merit badge materials, take initiative in learning, and demonstrate completion of each requirement.

## Steps to Begin

1. **Scoutmaster Approval:** Talk with your Scoutmaster first. Before starting any merit badge, Scouts must receive approval from the Scoutmaster.

2. **Blue Card:** Complete the blue card linked [here](files/blue_card.pdf), to obtain the Scoutmaster’s signature on the front of the card.

3. **Contact the Merit Badge Counselor:** Send an email to the counselor (Taeyoung Lee at [tylee.fdcl@gmail.com](mailto:tylee.fdcl@gmail.com)), including
    * Scout’s name
    * Merit badge requested
    * Parent or guardian copied on the email


4. **Schedule the First Meeting:** During the first meeeting, we will review
    * Merit badge requirements
    * Project expectations
    * Timeline for completion

5. **Work on Requirements:** Scouts should review the merit badge pamphlet and begin completing requirements.

6. **Completion:** Once all requirements are completed, Scounts should meet the Merit Badge Counselor to demonstrate that all of the requirements are satisfied. The counselor signs the blue card and records completion.

## Important Guidelines

* Parents or guardians should be copied on all communication.
* **Buddy system**. In accordance with BSA Youth Protection guidelines, Scouts must not meet one-on-one with an adult. When meeting with the Merit Badge Counselor, a Scout should be accompanied by a buddy (another Scout, a sibling, or a parent/guardian), or the meeting should take place in a group setting.
* Scouts must complete all requirements exactly as written in the official merit badge requirements.
* Some badges involve projects, demonstrations, or presentations.
* Scouts may work individually or in small groups depending on the activity.
