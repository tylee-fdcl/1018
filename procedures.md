# Procedure for Starting a Merit Badge

## Merit Badge Counselor

The role of a Merit Badge Counselor is to provide guidance, mentoring, and evaluation as Scouts work through the merit badge requirements. The counselor does not serve as a formal instructor. Scouts are expected to review the merit badge materials, take initiative in their learning, and demonstrate completion of each requirement.

## Steps to Begin

1. **Scoutmaster Approval:** Speak with your Scoutmaster first. Before starting any merit badge, Scouts must receive approval from the Scoutmaster.

2. **Blue Card:** Complete the blue card linked [here](files/blue_card.pdf) and obtain the Scoutmaster’s signature on the front.

3. **Contact the Merit Badge Counselor:** Send an email to the counselor (Taeyoung Lee at [tylee.fdcl@gmail.com](mailto:tylee.fdcl@gmail.com)), including:
   - Scout’s name  
   - Merit badge requested  
   - Parent or guardian copied on the email  

4. **Schedule the First Meeting:** During the first meeting, we will review:
   - Merit badge requirements  
   - Project expectations  
   - Timeline for completion  

5. **Work on Requirements:** Scouts should review the merit badge pamphlet and begin completing the requirements independently.

6. **Completion:** Once all requirements are completed, Scouts should meet with the Merit Badge Counselor to demonstrate completion. The counselor will sign the blue card and record the completion.

## Important Guidelines

- Parents or guardians must be copied on all communication.  
- **Buddy System:** In accordance with BSA Youth Protection guidelines, Scouts must not meet one-on-one with an adult. When meeting with the Merit Badge Counselor, a Scout must be accompanied by a buddy (another Scout, sibling, or parent/guardian), or the meeting must take place in a group setting.  
- Scouts must complete all requirements exactly as written in the official merit badge requirements.  
- Some badges involve projects, demonstrations, or presentations.  
- Scouts may work individually or in small groups, depending on the activity.
